# 天合光能 × OTGO AI行业深度交流 (2026-04-13)

**Format:** Informal lunch | **Duration:** ~1 hour
**Participants:** 辉哥 (OTGO CEO), 赵总 (天合光能 数字化负责人), Speaker 1

An external-facing conversation where 辉哥 articulated OTGO's product philosophy to an industrial enterprise audience. The discussion surfaced the genuine difficulty of AI adoption in manufacturing, contrasted big-model vs. small-model strategies, and explored knowledge management as the precondition for AI to work at all.

<div class="zh-trans">这是一次外部交流，辉哥向制造业客户阐述了 OTGO 的产品理念。对话聚焦于制造业 AI 落地的真实困境、大模型与小模型路线之争，以及知识管理作为 AI 落地前提条件的重要性。</div>

## Key Points

### 1. The AI Adoption Reality — Cost Paradox

MIT research: 95% of enterprise AI investments fail to produce measurable results. The core problem is not technology — it's that companies apply large models blindly without understanding the application context.

A counterexample: a company run by 95-后 returnees doing global KOL marketing with AI tools. Monthly salary ~¥3,000, but able to attract top talent *because* AI makes work genuinely enjoyable. This shows AI's real value: not just cost reduction, but enabling people to do more.

<div class="zh-trans">MIT报告：95% 的企业 AI 数字化投入未能取得效果。核心问题不是技术，而是企业盲目套用大模型、缺乏对应用场景的深入理解。反例：某出海 KOL 公司月薪3000仍能吸引优秀人才——因为 AI 让工作变得愉快，体现了 AI 真正的价值在于赋能而非降本。</div>

### 2. Big Model vs. Small Model — "Scaling Out" Not "Scaling Up"

Foundation models have already reached the level of humanity's smartest people in academic disciplines. Continuing to scale up is unnecessary. The future direction: industries use foundation model capabilities to train their own **domain-specific small models**.

<div class="zh-trans">基础模型能力已接近全人类最聪明人的学科水平，没必要继续无限扩大。未来方向：各行各业利用基础模型能力，训练各自的行业小模型。</div>

Three limitations of large models in enterprise settings:
1. Cannot directly "do" engineering work — industrial applications still require "world models" (unsolved)
2. Black box: cannot audit or fix when something goes wrong in mission-critical systems
3. AI coding problem: modifying one module causes unpredictable refactoring elsewhere

### 3. Mixture of Agents — The OTGO Architecture Answer

OTGO's answer to the black-box problem: decompose the large model into multiple small models, each responsible for a specific task (A, B, C…), each wrapped as an independent Agent. Agent-to-agent communication is observable by humans. Each agent's Memory and Skills are transparent.

This architecture (implemented as octo) is safer, more reliable, and more auditable than a single large model.

<div class="zh-trans">OTGO 的解答：将大模型拆分为多个小模型，每个负责特定任务，外层封装为独立 Agent。Agent 之间的通信可以被人类观察，每个 Agent 的 Memory 和 Skill 都透明可见。这就是 octo 的架构理念：安全、可靠、可审计。</div>

### 4. Knowledge Management — The Precondition

Knowledge = information + experience (华为's definition). The framework:
> Knowledge guides roles → roles guide actions → actions produce retrospectives → retrospectives distill experience → closes the loop back to knowledge

Three layers of knowledge (with difficulty increasing):

| Layer | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **Know What** | What something is | Concepts, terms, precise distinctions between screw types |
| **Know How** | How to do something | Procedures, steps, operational methods |
| **Know Why** | Why it was designed this way | The reasoning behind past decisions — hardest to capture, usually only in the master craftsman's head |

Know Why is the core of enterprise competitive advantage and the hardest to document. Example: 1M lines of open-source code show Know What and Know How, but not *why* certain architectural concepts were introduced.

Manufacturing has vast amounts of **tacit knowledge** — this is the real competitive moat. AI can help extract and preserve it, but only with deliberate knowledge management design.

<div class="zh-trans">知识管理的三个层次：Know What（是什么）→ Know How（怎么做）→ Know Why（为什么这样设计）。Know Why 是最核心也最难提炼的隐性知识，通常只在老师傅脑中。制造业的核心竞争力就藏在这里。</div>

### 5. Organization Change — Don't Just Cut Headcount

Using AI as a headcount-reduction tool is a strategic mistake. It creates fear, causes master craftsmen to withhold knowledge, and generates organizational resistance that makes AI adoption fail.

<div class="zh-trans">以 AI 为借口裁员是错误导向。会造成恐慌，让老师傅不配合，最终导致 AI 落地失败。</div>

The right sequence:
1. Redesign business processes and org structure first
2. Plan people development paths in advance (who to develop, new roles, who to transition)
3. Let AI help people accomplish things they couldn't do before — expand capability, don't just cut cost

**Agent responsibility principle:** Agents cannot bear responsibility for outcomes. Each agent must have a designated human owner. The owner is accountable for what the agent produces.

**"龙虾商业伦理学" (Lobster Business Ethics):** A proposed framework for ensuring AI applications protect workers rather than replace them. Goal: everyone benefits and becomes stronger through AI.

### 6. Token Cost Reality Check

| Reference Point | Cost |
|----------------|------|
| 大疆 (DJI) full-staff AI budget | ¥1,000+ per person per month |
| Top engineer at AI company | ~$8,000/day in token consumption |
| OTGO: original budget estimate | ¥100M/year |
| OTGO: revised estimate | ¥300–350M/year |

Token cost may exceed labor cost. Blind adoption without understanding consumption patterns is a serious risk.

<div class="zh-trans">token 成本可能比人力工资还贵：大疆全员 AI 预算每人每月千元起；某 AI 公司顶级工程师每天消耗 8000 美金 token。OTGO 年度预算从 1 亿追到 3-3.5 亿。盲目投入是真实风险。</div>

### 7. Broader Perspective — Technology and Society

- Technology grows exponentially; social adaptation grows logarithmically. The gap widens dangerously.
- Current moment analogous to the "Axial Age" (800–200 BCE): iron-age technology disrupted society but also produced great philosophers.
- Liberal arts / humanities are more valuable in the AI age — technical knowledge is easier to replace.
- AI should promote information symmetry → reduce irrational conflict and war.
- AI must not only benefit a few companies. "大拆小" (breaking big into small): every individual retains unique value.
